## SECTION 2: FEATURE 1 - PROMISE TO PAY (5 MINUTES)

### 2.1 THE PROBLEM (1 minute)

**Your words:**
"Right now, when a debtor offers to pay in installments, here's what typically happens:

1. Client calls and says 'I can pay R3,000 now and R2,000 a month for the rest'
2. The attorney notes the terms
3. Someone—usually a staff member—creates an agreement in Word
4. It gets printed, both parties sign it
5. Someone scans it and tries to file it properly
6. Hopefully it ends up in the right place

How long does that take? About 50 minutes per case. And we probably do this 20-25 times a month.

**The real problem:** No systematic record. No automation. And if there's a dispute later—'You said R2,000 a month, not R2,500'—we're hunting through files to find proof."

### 2.2 THE SOLUTION (2 minutes)

**Your words:**
"With the new system, here's what the attorney does instead:

1. Client calls with terms
2. Attorney opens the case in the system
3. Clicks 'Create Promise To Pay'
4. Enters the terms:
   - First payment: amount and date
   - Monthly payment: amount and day of month
   - Number of payments
5. Clicks 'Create'
6. Done.

That's 15 minutes instead of 50."

## SECTION 3: FEATURE 2 - GENERATE TASKS (5 MINUTES)

### 3.1 THE PROBLEM (1 minute)

**Your words:**
"The debt collection process has legal stages, right? Letter of Demand, Section 129 Notice, Summons, Judgment, Writ of Execution, possibly Sale in execution.

At each stage, there are specific steps that must be done in the right order. If you skip a step or do them out of order, the case can be delayed or we could have compliance problems.

Currently, the senior attorney has to remember all these steps and tell staff: 'Okay, for Summons stage, you need to draft the summons, file it at court, arrange service on the debtor, obtain proof of service, and set a reminder for the court date.'

**The problem:** Staff member has to write it all down. Sometimes they forget a step. Or they do steps in the wrong order. Cases get delayed. New staff members have to learn this process through trial and error."

### 3.2 THE SOLUTION (2 minutes)

**Your words:**
"When the attorney moves a case to a new stage, instead of giving verbal instructions, they click 'Generate Tasks.'

The system automatically creates a checklist of every required step, with:
- What needs to be done
- When it's due
- Who it's assigned to
- Priority level

For example, when moving to Summons stage, it creates:
1. Draft Summons Document — Due today (HIGH)
2. Submit to Court — Due tomorrow (HIGH)
3. Arrange Service on Debtor — Due in 5 days (HIGH)
4. Obtain Proof of Service — Due in 7 days (HIGH)
5. Set Court Date Reminder — Due in 14 days (NORMAL)

All these tasks appear on the staff member's to-do list. They follow the list, check boxes as they complete items, and the case moves through systematically.

No step gets missed. Nothing falls through the cracks."

**[DEMO HERE if possible]**
- Open a matter
- Show current stage
- Click Generate Tasks
- Show the checklist of auto-generated tasks
- Show that they appear in staff member's to-do list

### 3.3 WHY THIS MATTERS (1.5 minutes)

**Your words:**
"Three big benefits here:

**First, compliance:** Every legal requirement at each stage is documented. If we're ever audited or questioned about our process, we have proof: 'Here's every step we take at the Summons stage, and here's proof we did all of them.'

**Second, speed:** Cases move through the pipeline faster because nothing gets delayed by a forgotten step. What used to take 4-5 days now takes 2-3 days consistently.

**Third, training:** When you have a new attorney or paralegal, instead of training them by sitting next to them for weeks, they see the task list. They learn the process immediately. This is how we do things here.'

Also, this removes the burden on the senior attorney to remember everything. Senior attorneys can focus on legal strategy and client relationships, not on remembering process checklists."

## SECTION 4: FEATURE 3 - WHATSAPP MESSAGING (5 MINUTES)

### 4.1 THE PROBLEM (1 minute)

**Your words:**
"Right now, when we need to contact a debtor, someone calls them from their personal phone or sends an email.

Here's the problem: there's no official record.

If we call Mr. Johnson and say 'Your payment is due Friday, we need R5,000,' and he later says 'Nobody told me!' we have no proof. We can't show:
- When we contacted him
- What we said
- Whether he even received the message

And if we end up in a dispute or court case, and the debtor claims 'BAM Attorneys never told me I had to pay by Friday,' we literally have nothing to show the judge.

**From a legal standpoint, this is a risk.**"

### 4.2 THE SOLUTION (2 minutes)

**Your words:**
"With the new system, we send messages through the case management system, integrated with WhatsApp.

Debtors prefer WhatsApp—it's personal, immediate—so they engage better. But from our side, we have complete documentation.

Every message is recorded with:
- Content of the message
- Date and time sent
- Status: Sent → Delivered → Read (with exact timestamp of when they read it)
- Any response from the debtor
- All stored permanently in the case file

The system has templates for common messages:
- 'Payment Reminder'
- 'Arrangement Offer'
- Or staff can write custom messages

But everything goes through the system, so everything is logged."

**Your words:**

## SECTION 7: RISK MANAGEMENT (3 MINUTES)

### 7.1 Addressing Common Concerns

**If they ask: "What if staff doesn't use it?"**

Response: "Fair concern. That's why we're doing a pilot first. If we see low adoption, we either adjust the system or adjust training. But I'm confident adoption will be high because the system makes their jobs easier, not harder. The Promise to Pay feature alone is a 35-minute time saver."

**If they ask: "Is this secure? Are we putting debtors' info at risk?"**

Response: "Security is built in from the ground up. Everything is encrypted. Backups are automatic. The system is designed to meet legal and compliance standards—it has to, because we're handling sensitive debtor information. Honestly, it's more secure than having staff handle communications on personal phones with no central record."

**If they ask: "What if the technology breaks?"**

Response: "We have customer support and automatic backups. Plus, this isn't complicated technology—it's essentially automated forms and messaging. There's very little that can break. And if something does, we have processes to revert to manual procedures while it's being fixed. It won't disrupt work."

**If they ask: "What if debtors don't like WhatsApp?"**

Response: "Research shows 80%+ of professionals prefer WhatsApp for business communication. We've tested sample messages and got immediate positive responses. Plus, this is optional—we're offering it as a tool. If a particular debtor doesn't have WhatsApp, we still have email and phone. But the data suggests debtors will prefer it."

**If they ask: "What about privacy? Sending messages through a system?"**

Response: "WhatsApp itself is end-to-end encrypted. The system logs the message in our secure server. This is actually more professional and compliant than staff texting from personal phones with no record. We're not recording calls or reading messages—we're just logging that messages were sent, delivered, and read. That's legally standard for business communication."

### 7.2 Risks Mitigated

**Your words:**
"Let me address the main risks upfront:

| Risk | How We Mitigate |
|------|-----------------|
| Staff resistance | Easy-to-use system + real time savings makes adoption natural |
| Technical issues | Automatic backups + customer support + rollback procedures |
| Compliance problems | System is built to legal standards + audit trail of everything |
| Data security | Encryption + secure servers + controlled access |
| Debtors not engaging | WhatsApp is industry standard + we still have email/phone options |
| ROI doesn't materialize | We measure and report; can adjust or pause if needed |

Bottom line: we've thought through the risks and built in safeguards."

## FINAL TIPS FOR SUCCESS

1. **Stay calm and confident** - You know this system. It works.
2. **Answer their questions, don't over-explain** - If they ask, answer. Don't give a 10-minute speech unless they ask.
3. **Use real examples** - Instead of abstract numbers, reference actual cases: "Like that Mrs. Ndlela matter we discussed..."
4. **Don't use tech jargon** - If you catch yourself saying something technical, rephrase it in business terms
5. **Show, don't tell** - Live demo is infinitely better than explaining. Have screenshots ready as backup.
6. **Be honest about limitations** - "This won't solve everything" is more credible than "This is perfect"
7. **Focus on business value, not technology** - This isn't about cool tech, it's about working smarter
8. **Respect their time** - Stay on schedule. If you said 30 minutes, be done in 30 minutes.
