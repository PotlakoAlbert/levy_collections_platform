# PRESENTATION DAY CHECKLIST
## Use This the Day of Your Boss Meeting

---

## WEEK BEFORE MEETING

**Logistics:**
- [ ] Schedule meeting with boss (confirm date/time)
- [ ] Choose quiet meeting room (1 hour minimum)
- [ ] Check that meeting room has: table, chairs, good lighting
- [ ] Ensure no interruptions (ask admin to hold calls)
- [ ] Test WiFi in meeting room (if presenting from laptop)

**Technology Setup:**
- [ ] Open Levy Collection Manager on your laptop
- [ ] Test all three features work correctly
- [ ] Have test matter ready (so you don't waste time finding one)
- [ ] Take screenshots as backup (if system doesn't work)
- [ ] Save screenshots to folder on desktop
- [ ] Download & save all presentation documents locally

**Materials:**
- [ ] Print 2 copies: 1-page executive summary
- [ ] Print 1 copy: Full diagrams document
- [ ] Print 1 copy: Quick reference card (for your eyes only)
- [ ] Print 1 copy: One-page handout (to leave with boss)
- [ ] Get a quality pen (blue or black)
- [ ] Get notepad in case they want to take notes

**Personal Preparation:**
- [ ] Reread the presentation script once
- [ ] Memorize opening statement (2 sentences)
- [ ] Memorize key numbers: 7,300% ROI, 360 hours/year, R500,000+
- [ ] Practice explaining each feature out loud (5 minutes each)
- [ ] Anticipate 3 possible objections and your responses
- [ ] Prepare 1-minute elevator pitch (just in case)
- [ ] Get good sleep night before
- [ ] Eat a good breakfast morning of presentation

---

## DAY OF PRESENTATION - MORNING

**Before work:**
- [ ] Check weather (allows time for travel)
- [ ] Choose professional outfit (what you'd normally wear to meet with boss)
- [ ] Leave extra time for traffic/parking
- [ ] Bring the printed materials in a folder
- [ ] Bring your laptop + charger
- [ ] Bring this checklist (for reference in waiting area)

**At your desk (30 minutes before meeting):**
- [ ] Reread quick reference card (2 minutes)
- [ ] Open Levy Collection Manager, navigate to test matter
- [ ] Make sure all documents are printed and in order
- [ ] Check laptop battery (plug in if needed)
- [ ] Use restroom (no interruptions during presentation)
- [ ] Get a glass of water
- [ ] Take 3 deep breaths
- [ ] Remind yourself: "I know this system. This is good for the practice."

---

## WALKING TO THE MEETING

**Mental checklist:**
- [ ] Am I calm and confident? (Yes)
- [ ] Do I have all materials? (Check folder)
- [ ] Is my laptop with me? (Check)
- [ ] Do I know the opening statement? (Yes)
- [ ] Am I ready to demo features? (Yes)
- [ ] Do I have screenshot backups? (Yes)

---

## SETTING UP THE MEETING ROOM

**When you arrive (5 minutes early):**
- [ ] Arrange chairs: You and boss at table, not across from each other
- [ ] Position laptop so boss can see screen clearly
- [ ] Place printed summary + diagrams on table
- [ ] Put pen and notepad within reach
- [ ] Close any distracting windows/tabs on laptop
- [ ] Silence your phone (do not vibrate)
- [ ] Take a seat and arrange materials neatly

---

## PRESENTATION FLOW - CHECK AS YOU GO

**Opening (2 minutes):**
- [ ] Greet them professionally
- [ ] Thank them for their time
- [ ] Deliver opening statement
- [ ] Ask if they're ready to begin

**Feature 1: Promise to Pay (5 minutes):**
- [ ] Explain the problem (50 min manual process)
- [ ] Explain the solution (15 min automated process)
- [ ] Live demo OR show screenshots
- [ ] Emphasize legal protection benefit
- [ ] Stop and check: "Questions so far?"

**Feature 2: Generate Tasks (5 minutes):**
- [ ] Explain the problem (missed steps, delays)
- [ ] Explain the solution (auto-generated task lists)
- [ ] Live demo OR show screenshots
- [ ] Emphasize compliance and training benefits
- [ ] Stop and check: "Make sense?"

**Feature 3: WhatsApp Messaging (5 minutes):**
- [ ] Explain the problem (no record of communications)
- [ ] Explain the solution (tracked messages in system)
- [ ] Live demo OR show screenshots
- [ ] Emphasize legal protection and effectiveness
- [ ] Stop and check: "Any questions?"

**The Numbers (5 minutes):**
- [ ] Present time savings (30 hours/month)
- [ ] Present revenue impact (R500,000+)
- [ ] Present ROI (7,300%)
- [ ] Say it confidently

**Implementation (3 minutes):**
- [ ] Outline timeline (Week 1-4)
- [ ] Mention measurement (60-day report)
- [ ] Offer options (full rollout vs. pilot vs. smaller pilot)

**Closing (3 minutes):**
- [ ] Summarize briefly
- [ ] Ask for decision
- [ ] Listen to their response
- [ ] Don't push if they hesitate

---

## HANDLING DIFFERENT SCENARIOS

**If system works perfectly:**
- [ ] Demo all three features
- [ ] Let them play with the system a bit if interested
- [ ] Offer to do a second demo after meeting if needed

**If system crashes/doesn't work:**
- [ ] Calmly say: "Let me show you the screenshots instead"
- [ ] Show printed screenshots
- [ ] Offer: "Happy to do a live demo after we get this sorted"
- [ ] Continue presentation—screenshots work fine

**If they interrupt with questions:**
- [ ] Answer directly and briefly
- [ ] Don't give a 10-minute lecture unless they ask for it
- [ ] Ask: "Does that answer your question?"
- [ ] Get back to presentation

**If they seem uninterested:**
- [ ] Pause and ask: "Is now a good time, or should we reschedule?"
- [ ] Don't push forward if they're distracted
- [ ] Better to reschedule than present to someone not engaged

**If they ask about competitors/similar systems:**
- [ ] Honestly answer if you know
- [ ] If not: "That's a good question. Let me research that and get back to you"
- [ ] Don't make up information

**If they ask very technical questions:**
- [ ] Translate to business terms
- [ ] If you don't know: "Good question. Let me get the technical details and send them to you"
- [ ] Don't pretend to know more than you do

---

## AFTER PRESENTATION

**If they say YES:**
- [ ] Confirm these exact points:
  1. When does staff training happen?
  2. Who's in the pilot team?
  3. When can we start?
  4. When do you want to hear back (60 days)?
- [ ] Write these down
- [ ] Send thank you email within 1 hour
- [ ] Schedule staff training immediately
- [ ] Start prep for Week 1

**If they say MAYBE or NEED MORE TIME:**
- [ ] Ask: "What questions remain? What would help you decide?"
- [ ] Listen carefully to their concern
- [ ] Offer follow-up options:
  - Written analysis of specific concern
  - Smaller pilot to prove concept
  - Follow-up meeting in [X weeks]
- [ ] Don't push
- [ ] Send thank you email, confirm next steps

**If they say NO:**
- [ ] Don't push back
- [ ] Ask: "Help me understand the concern"
- [ ] Listen to their reason
- [ ] Don't argue
- [ ] Ask: "Would it be okay to revisit this in [X months]?"
- [ ] Send polite email acknowledging their decision
- [ ] Ask: "Is there anything I can do to make this more appealing?"

**Regardless:**
- [ ] Leave one copy of 1-page handout with them
- [ ] Collect any handwritten notes they made
- [ ] Thank them again
- [ ] Close the door quietly as you leave

---

## AFTER YOU LEAVE THE MEETING

**Within 1 hour:**
- [ ] Send professional thank you email
- [ ] Confirm decisions/next steps from the meeting
- [ ] Include any follow-up information they requested

**Within 24 hours:**
- [ ] Follow through on any commitments you made
- [ ] Send any promised information
- [ ] Schedule follow-up meetings (if needed)
- [ ] Begin prep for next steps (if approved)

**If approved - Immediate actions:**
- [ ] Create staff training agenda
- [ ] Prepare training materials
- [ ] Schedule all-hands training meeting
- [ ] Set calendar reminder for 60-day report
- [ ] Begin pilot prep

---

## THINGS NOT TO DO

**During the presentation:**
- [ ] ❌ Don't use technical jargon
- [ ] ❌ Don't read from a script (use it as guide only)
- [ ] ❌ Don't talk too fast (breathe, pause, speak clearly)
- [ ] ❌ Don't show all windows/tabs on laptop (looks messy)
- [ ] ❌ Don't check your phone (put it away)
- [ ] ❌ Don't admit you're nervous
- [ ] ❌ Don't make excuses before presenting
- [ ] ❌ Don't over-explain if they seem to understand
- [ ] ❌ Don't badmouth the current process too much
- [ ] ❌ Don't promise things you can't deliver

---

## EMERGENCY BACKUP CONTACT

**If something goes wrong:**

Can't reach boss at meeting time:
- [ ] Call office immediately (5 min before meeting time)
- [ ] Email asking to reschedule
- [ ] Stay available in case they want to meet later

Laptop completely dies:
- [ ] No panic
- [ ] Use printed screenshots
- [ ] Offer: "I can do a live demo when we get this sorted"
- [ ] Presentation works fine with just screenshots

Power goes out:
- [ ] Walk to best lit area
- [ ] Use printed diagrams
- [ ] Continue with verbal explanations
- [ ] Offer follow-up demo when power returns

They have to leave early:
- [ ] Quickly summarize (30-second version)
- [ ] Leave printed one-page handout
- [ ] Ask when you can follow up
- [ ] Confirm next meeting time

## TIME KEEPING

**Keep track of time (print a watch/clock if needed):**

Target times:
- Opening: 0-2 min
- Feature 1: 2-7 min
- Feature 2: 7-12 min
- Feature 3: 12-17 min
- Numbers: 17-22 min
- Implementation: 22-25 min
- Closing: 25-28 min
- Questions: 28-35 min

If you're running long:
- Cut the numbers section slightly (they'll remember key ones)
- Skip detailed implementation timeline
- Offer more detail in follow-up

If you're done early:
- Ask: "Do you have questions about anything I covered?"
- Don't just leave—fill the silence

## FINAL REMINDERS

1. **Breathe** - You've got this
2. **Smile** - You're excited about this, it shows
3. **Listen** - Their questions tell you what matters
4. **Clarify** - Ask "Does that make sense?" to confirm understanding
5. **Respect** - Their time, their concerns, their decision-making
6. **Follow up** - Do what you said you'd do
7. **Measure** - Track actual results, learn from them
8. **Adjust** - If something isn't working, say so and fix it
