# PRESENTATION QUICK REFERENCE CARD
## For Presenting to Your Boss - Print This & Keep Handy

---

## OPENING STATEMENT (Memorize This)

**"Thanks for making time. I want to show you three automation features we've built for the Levy Collection Manager. These are designed to take manual, repetitive work off our staff's plates and help us collect debt faster and more reliably. Three features, about 5 minutes each. Questions anytime—nothing technical, I promise. The bottom line: working smarter, not harder."**

---

## FEATURE 1: PROMISE TO PAY - KEY TALKING POINTS

**THE PROBLEM (Say this):**
- Current: 50 minutes per case (debtor call → staff creates Word doc → print/sign/scan/file)
- 25 cases/month = 20 hours/month wasted
- Risk: No proof of agreed terms if dispute

**THE SOLUTION (Say this):**
- Attorney enters terms in 5 minutes
- System creates agreement, emails to debtor, stores in file
- Total: 15 minutes per case
- **Time saved: 35 minutes × 25 cases = 14.6 hours/month**

**KEY BENEFIT TO EMPHASIZE:**
- "The real value is legal protection. If we're ever in court or a dispute, we have a timestamped agreement. We can prove exactly what was promised."

**IF THEY ASK:** "Is this secure?"
- **Answer:** "Everything is encrypted and backed up. It meets all legal requirements. It's actually more secure than staff handling things on personal phones."

---

## FEATURE 2: GENERATE TASKS - KEY TALKING POINTS

**THE PROBLEM (Say this):**
- Legal process has stages: LOD, S129, Summons, Judgment, Writ, Sale
- At each stage, specific steps must be done in order
- Currently: Senior attorney tells staff verbally
- Risk: Steps get missed or done out of order → delays and compliance issues

**THE SOLUTION (Say this):**
- Attorney moves case to new stage
- Clicks "Generate Tasks"
- System auto-creates checklist with all required steps, due dates, who it's assigned to
- Example (Summons): 5 tasks created automatically with specific due dates

**KEY BENEFIT TO EMPHASIZE:**
- "Two things: (1) No steps get missed—we maintain compliance. (2) New staff learn the process automatically just by following the checklist. No need for weeks of training."

**IF THEY ASK:** "Will staff actually use this?"
- **Answer:** "Yes, because it makes their jobs easier. They won't have to worry 'Did I forget something?' It's all there."

---

## FEATURE 3: WHATSAPP MESSAGING - KEY TALKING POINTS

**THE PROBLEM (Say this):**
- Right now: Staff call/email debtors from personal phones or personal emails
- No official record of conversation
- If debtor says "Nobody told me I had to pay by Friday," we have no proof
- Creates legal risk

**THE SOLUTION (Say this):**
- Send messages through the system
- Integrated with WhatsApp (debtors prefer it)
- System records: content, date, time, delivery status, read status
- Everything stays in case file permanently

**KEY BENEFIT TO EMPHASIZE:**
- "From a legal standpoint, this is huge. If we're in court and the judge asks 'When was the debtor notified?' we can show: 'Here's the message, sent May 3 at 2:15 PM, delivered, and they read it at 2:47 PM.' That's evidence."

**IF THEY ASK:** "What about privacy?"
- **Answer:** "WhatsApp is end-to-end encrypted. We're just logging that messages were sent, delivered, and read. This is actually more professional and compliant than personal texts with no record."

## HANDLING OBJECTIONS - QUICK RESPONSES

**"I'm worried staff won't use it"**
→ "The Promise to Pay feature alone saves 35 minutes per case. Staff will want to use it. Plus, the pilot approach lets us see adoption early."

**"What if something breaks?"**
→ "Automatic backups, customer support, and easy rollback. Plus this is simple technology—forms and messaging. Very stable."

**"Is it secure?"**
→ "Encrypted, backed up, meets legal standards. More secure than personal phones with no record."

**"Will debtors like WhatsApp?"**
→ "80%+ prefer it to email. We've tested it. Debtors respond immediately."

**"I need more time to think about it"**
→ "That's fair. What would help you feel more comfortable? More information? Or would a smaller pilot—one staff member, 5 cases for 2 weeks—help?"

**"Can we do a smaller pilot first?"**
→ "Perfect, that's actually what we were planning. Let's do even smaller: one person, one case type, 5 real cases for 2 weeks. Then we report back."

## IF THEY SAY YES - Immediate Next Steps

**Confirm these four things:**
1. "Staff training when?" (Suggest specific date)
2. "Who should be in the pilot?" (Team A probably)
3. "When can we start pilot?" (Week 2)
4. "When can I report back to you?" (60 days)

**Then send thank you email within the hour with these details.**

---

## IF THEY SAY NO - Don't Push

**Ask clarifying questions:**
- "I appreciate the hesitation. Help me understand—what's the main concern?"
- "Is it the cost? The risk? The timing? Something else?"
- "What would make you more comfortable moving forward?"

**Then listen and address their actual concern.**

---

- One case type (LOD to Summons cases)

- Two weeks of trial
- Then we report back with results

If it works, we scale up. If there are issues, we fix them. Fair?"

## KEY PHRASES TO USE

**Use these phrases—they're lawyer-friendly:**
- "Legal protection" (not "compliance")
- "Documented proof" (not "data logging")
- "Audit trail" (not "record keeping")
- "Risk mitigation" (not "we won't get sued")
- "Workflow automation" (not "cool tech")
- "Systematic approach" (not "automated")

**Avoid tech jargon:**
- Don't say "API" or "backend" or "database"
- Don't explain the technology—explain the business outcome
- If you catch yourself using jargon, rephrase in business terms

---

## TONE GUIDELINES

**Sound like this:**
- Confident (you know this system)
- Professional (not excited, not sales-y)
- Respectful of their time (stay on schedule)
- Open to questions (welcome interruptions)
- Honest about benefits (not overselling)

**Don't sound like this:**
- Salesy or pushy
- Technical or nerdy
- Uncertain or apologetic
- Like this is your first time presenting
- Like you're hiding something

## WHAT TO DO WITH YOUR HANDS

- Print out the one-page summary + diagrams
- Have them on the table, not waving them around
- Open the system on laptop—don't try to hold it
- Use a pointer or cursor to show things
- Look at them when talking (not at laptop screen)
- Pause and breathe—don't rush

## EMERGENCY BACKUP PLAN

**If system crashes during presentation:**

1. Don't panic, don't apologize excessively
2. Say: "Let me pull up a screenshot instead"
3. Show printed screenshots (have these ready)
4. Continue as normal
5. Offer: "I'm happy to do a live demo after we get this technical issue sorted"
6. The presentation still works with screenshots—the system is just bonus

## THE GOAL

Your goal is not to convince them on the spot. Your goal is to:
✓ Give them accurate information
✓ Show them this is well-thought-out
✓ Demonstrate the business value
✓ Answer their concerns
✓ Ask for permission to proceed

If they say yes great. If they want more info, that's fine too.
