# Speaker's Notes - How to Present to Your Boss
## A Guide for Presenting the Three Features

---

## BEFORE THE MEETING

### Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Review the Executive Summary (1 page version)
- [ ] Practice the presentation (10-15 minutes)
- [ ] Open the system on a laptop for demo
- [ ] Print summary + diagrams documents
- [ ] Have ROI numbers ready (memorize the big ones)
- [ ] Test that WhatsApp, Promise to Pay, and Tasks features work
- [ ] Take screenshots if the system isn't available

### What to Wear
- Professional attire (same as normal work)
- Don't dress differently - this is a business update

### Setting
- Meeting room, quiet space
- Laptop/projector ready if showing live demo
- Not during their busy time
- Allow 30-45 minutes

---

## THE PRESENTATION FLOW (30 Minutes)

### OPENING (2 minutes)
**Your words:**
"Thanks for taking the time. I want to show you three automation features we've built for the Levy Collection Manager that will save us significant time and help us collect debt faster. There are three features, each takes about 5 minutes to explain. Any questions, just interrupt me."

*Why this works:* 
- Shows respect for their time
- Sets expectations (3 features, 5 min each)
- Invites questions (they feel heard)

---

### FEATURE 1: PROMISE TO PAY (5 minutes)

**START with the problem (1 minute):**
"Currently, when a debtor offers to pay in installments, here's what happens:
- Staff member notes it down
- Attorney creates an agreement in Word
- Someone prints it, both sign it
- It gets scanned and filed
- Everyone hopes it's in the right place

This takes about 50 minutes per case. We do about 25 of these per month, so that's 20 hours of work per month just creating documents."

**SHOW the solution (2 minutes):**
"With the new system, the attorney enters the terms directly:
- First payment amount and date
- Monthly payment amount and day
- Number of payments
- Click 'Create'

Done. System creates the formal agreement, emails it to the debtor, stores it in the case file with a timestamp. Takes 15 minutes instead of 50. We save 35 minutes per case × 25 cases = 875 minutes per month = about 15 hours per month."

**DEMO if possible (1 minute):**
"Let me show you what it looks like..." [Open the system, click through]

**HIGHLIGHT the benefit (1 minute):**
"The real value here isn't just time savings - it's legal protection. If a debtor later claims 'we never discussed installments,' we have a timestamped digital agreement. This protects us."

---

### FEATURE 2: GENERATE TASKS (5 minutes)

**START with the problem (1 minute):**
"The debt collection process has legal stages: Letter of Demand, Section 129 Notice, Summons, Judgment, Writ, Sale. At each stage, there are specific steps that must be done in order, or we risk non-compliance.

Currently, the senior attorney must remember all these steps and verbally tell staff. Sometimes steps get missed. We end up with cases delayed because someone forgot to arrange service."

**SHOW the solution (2 minutes):**
"When the attorney moves a case to a new stage, they click 'Generate Tasks.' The system automatically creates a checklist of all required steps, assigns them to staff members with due dates and priorities.

For example, moving to Summons stage creates 5 tasks automatically:
1. Draft Summons (Due: Today)
2. Submit to Court (Due: Day 2)
3. Arrange Service (Due: Day 5)
4. Obtain Proof (Due: Day 7)
5. Set Court Reminder (Due: Day 14)

Staff sees these in their task list, completes them in order, cases move systematically through the process."

**DEMO if possible (1 minute):**
"Let me show you what the task list looks like..." [Open system]

**HIGHLIGHT the benefit (1 minute):**
"Two real benefits: (1) No steps get missed - we maintain compliance. (2) New staff learn the process automatically by following the task lists. We don't need to spend hours training people."

---

### FEATURE 3: WHATSAPP MESSAGING (5 minutes)

**START with the problem (1 minute):**
"When we need to contact a debtor, we typically call them from a personal phone or send an email. But here's the problem: no record of the conversation.

If a debtor later says 'Nobody told me I had to pay by Friday,' we have no proof. And if we end up in a dispute or court case, we can't show what we communicated to them. This creates legal and compliance risk."

**SHOW the solution (2 minutes):**
"With the new system, we send messages through the case management system. It's integrated with WhatsApp - so debtors receive WhatsApp messages, which they prefer.

But here's the key difference: we have complete records.
- When it was sent
- When it was delivered
- When it was read
- The exact content
- All stored in the case file

The system suggests templates like 'Payment Reminder' or 'Arrangement Offer' so staff doesn't have to write from scratch."

**DEMO if possible (1 minute):**
"Let me show you the message history..." [Open system]

**HIGHLIGHT the benefit (1 minute):**
"The legal protection is huge. If we're ever in court defending our collection actions, we can show: 'Here's when we contacted Mr. Johnson, here's the exact message, here's proof he read it.' That's powerful evidence."

---

### THE NUMBERS (8 minutes)

**TIME SAVINGS (2 minutes):**
"Let's quantify the time savings:
- Promise to Pay: 35 min saved × 25 cases/month = 14.6 hours/month
- Manual reminders: 10 min × 50 interactions/month = 8.3 hours/month  
- Documentation: 15 min × 25 cases/month = 6.3 hours/month

Total: About 30 hours per month saved. That's about R15,000 per month in staff time recovered.

Annually: 360 hours saved = R180,000 in recovered staff capacity."

**ADDITIONAL REVENUE (2 minutes):**
"WhatsApp messages get 80% read rate versus 20% for emails. This means faster responses from debtors.

We estimate:
- 15-20% faster debt collection = R9,000-12,000 per month faster
- 25% more payment arrangements agreed due to easier system
- Better tracking prevents payment mishaps

Conservative estimate: R500,000 in additional annual revenue."

**COST (1 minute):**
"The system costs about R15,000 per year. So:
- Savings: R180,000 staff time
- Revenue: R500,000+ additional collection
- Cost: R15,000

ROI: We gain R680,000 on a R15,000 investment. That's 7,300% return."

**BOTTOM LINE (1 minute):**
"This isn't about fancy technology. It's about basic automation that frees up staff to do higher-value work and helps us collect debt faster. Every dollar we invest returns 73 dollars."

---

### IMPLEMENTATION (3 minutes)

**TIMELINE (1.5 minutes):**
"Week 1: Setup and staff training (one hour session)
Week 2-3: Pilot with one team - we monitor and fix any issues
Week 4: All teams begin using it
Month 2-3: Full integration with our existing systems

This is not disruptive. We're doing a careful pilot first."

**WHAT WE NEED FROM YOU (1 minute):**
"Honestly, just your approval to proceed. We've built this, it works, it's ready. We're asking permission to train the staff and start using it.

We're planning to start training next week. Once you say yes, we're ready to go."

**MEASUREMENT (0.5 minutes):**
"In 60 days, we'll report back with actual numbers:
- How many hours did we actually save?
- What was the revenue impact?
- What challenges did we face?
- Any adjustments needed?

We're not asking you to approve this forever - we're asking permission to try it for 3 months with measurement."

---

### CLOSING (2 minutes)

**HANDLE OBJECTIONS (1 minute):**
Anticipate what they might ask:

*If they say: "I'm not sure the debtors will like WhatsApp"*
Response: "Research shows 80%+ of professionals prefer WhatsApp for business. We've tested with sample messages and got immediate responses."

*If they say: "Is it secure?"*
Response: "Everything is encrypted and backed up. Our team has ensured it meets all legal and compliance requirements. Honestly, it's more secure than emails on personal phones."

*If they say: "What if staff doesn't use it?"*
Response: "We'll make sure of adoption by making it easier to use than not using it. The Promise to Pay feature alone saves 35 minutes per case - staff will want to use it."

**THE ASK (1 minute):**
"So here's what I'm asking: Can we proceed with the implementation plan?
- Week 1: Training
- Week 2-3: Pilot
- Report back in 60 days with results

If you're hesitant, we can do an even smaller pilot with just one staff member and one case type first. But I believe this is ready."

## HANDLING DIFFERENT BOSS PERSONALITY TYPES

### If Your Boss is a "Risk Manager"
Focus on:
- Legal compliance and audit trail
- Risk reduction (documented proof)
- Phased rollout (start with pilot)
- Conservative projections (underpromise, overdeliver)

Say: "This actually reduces our legal risk. We'll have documented proof of all communications."

### If Your Boss is a "Financial Optimizer"
Focus on:
- ROI numbers (7,300%)
- Cost savings (R180,000/year)
- Revenue increase (R500,000+)
- Payback period (system pays for itself in weeks)

Say: "From a pure financial perspective, this is a no-brainer. Even at 50% of projected benefit, it's still a 3,000% ROI."

### If Your Boss is an "Operations Person"
Focus on:
- Process improvements (systematic workflow)
- Consistency (same process every time)
- Compliance (nothing gets missed)
- Measurability (we'll track everything)

Say: "This brings consistency to our process. No variations, no missed steps, everything measured."

### If Your Boss is a "People Person"
Focus on:
- Staff will like it (less busy work)
- Better training for new staff
- Clear accountability
- Work-life balance improvements

Say: "Our staff will appreciate this. They spend too much time on administrative tasks. This frees them to do work they actually enjoy."

## WHAT TO DO IF THEY SAY "NO"

**Don't push back.** Instead, ask clarifying questions:

"I appreciate the concern. Can you help me understand what specifically is holding you back? Is it:
- Cost? (We can show the ROI again)
- Risk? (We can address specific concerns)
- Timing? (We can schedule for a better time)
- Something else?"

**If they want more time to think:**
"That's fair. What questions can I answer for you in the meantime? What would make you more comfortable moving forward?"

**If they want a pilot:**
"Perfect! That's actually what we were planning anyway. Let's start with just one staff member and 5 cases, see how it goes, and report back in 2 weeks."

---

## WHAT TO DO IF THEY SAY "YES"

**Confirm next steps immediately:**
"Great! Here's what happens next:
1. We'll send out a meeting invite for staff training (week 1)
2. We'll brief the team on the features
3. We'll do a supervised pilot with Team A starting week 2
4. I'll report back to you weekly for the first month

Is there anything you want me to include in the staff briefing?"

**Send a thank you email within the hour:**
"Thanks for approving the implementation of the new Levy Collection Manager features. We're excited to get started. I've scheduled staff training for [date]. I'll check in with you weekly and deliver a full report in 60 days."

## AFTER THE PRESENTATION

**If you have time:**
- Offer a live demonstration
- Show real case examples
- Print key documents for them to review

**Follow up within 24 hours:**
- Send email summarizing the meeting
- Include decision points and next steps
- Include contact info if they have questions

**Be ready to adjust:**
- If they want to modify the timeline, be flexible
- If they want a smaller pilot, agree
- Show you're listening to their concerns

---

## FINAL TIPS

1. **Be confident but not arrogant** - You've done the work, you know this will work
2. **Use their language** - For lawyers, talk about "legal protection," "compliance," "documentation"
3. **Show, don't just tell** - Open the system and show it working
4. **Numbers matter** - Have the ROI calculation ready
5. **Simplify** - Avoid technical jargon, explain like they're a client
6. **Be ready for questions** - The more they ask, the more interested they are
7. **Respect their time** - Deliver in 30 minutes, not 45
8. **Have everything printed** - Don't rely on showing everything on screen
9. **Walk them through visually** - Diagrams are more persuasive than words
10. **Leave them with documentation** - They may want to read more later
